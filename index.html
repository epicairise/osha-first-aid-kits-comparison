Frequently Asked Questions

How do I choose the right OSHA-compliant first aid kit for my team size?

OSHA recommends tailoring first aid kits based on team size and workplace hazards. For example, a 10-person kit suits small teams, while larger teams may require kits supporting 50 or 100 employees.

What's the difference between ANSI Class A and Class B first aid kits?

Class A kits are designed for common workplace injuries, suitable for low-risk environments. Class B kits cater to high-risk workplaces, including additional items like tourniquets and splints.

Are there specific first aid kits for trades like construction or chemical plants?

Yes, certain kits are tailored for specific trades. For instance, kits with eyewash stations are ideal for chemical exposure, while construction sites may require kits with trauma pads and burn treatments.
